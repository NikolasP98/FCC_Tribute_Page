A brief history of Nelson Mandela's life:

Nelson Mandela was born on July 18, 1918 within the Thembu tribe in the South African village of Mvezo. Here he was raised and recieved formal education in a missionary school.
Nelson excelled in his grades and got involved in politics later in his life. He knew that people of color were being abused in his country, so he stopped at nothing to change this.
Shortly after, Mandela was arrested by the South African government, controlled by the Apartheid. He spent the next 27 years in prison.
Many rebellions and uprisings took place during his time in prison. These desparate acts of freedom bacame more intense as time went on. Nelson Mandela became the symbol of freedom in South Africa, where citizens fought for his freedom.
Nelson was freed in 1990, at the age of 71. His release meant the fall of Apartheid and the rise of a multiculturale regime.
4 years after his release, he was elected president by the people. He is recognized as the first black leader of this country. After serving his years in South Africa's presidency, Mandela settled down and died in 2013 from a lung infection.

Here is a small list of Nelson Mandela's accomplishments:

  • 1993 - Earned the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1994 - Elected President of South Africa
  • 1995 - Hosted the Rugby World Cup
  • 1999 - Established the Nelson Mandela Foundation
  • 2003 - Established the Mandela Rhodes Foundation
